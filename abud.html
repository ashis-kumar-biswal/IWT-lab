Mount Abu, the only hill station in Rajasthan, is renowned for its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and cultural significance, making it a popular retreat in the desert region of India. Geographical Features Mount Abu is situated in the Sirohi district of Rajasthan, at an elevation of approximately 1,220 meters (4,003 feet) above sea level, making it the highest peak in the Aravalli Range. The hill station is characterized by its rocky plateau, lush green forests, serene lakes, and waterfalls, often referred to as an "oasis in the desert" due to its contrasting environment with the surrounding arid landscape. Wikipedia +1 Historical Significance Historically, Mount Abu has been a significant site for both Jain and Hindu religions. It is believed to be the retreat of Sage Vashistha, who performed a yajna (sacrificial ritual) here, seeking divine intervention for righteousness on earth. The name "Abu" is derived from the ancient name "Arbuda," linked to a legend involving a serpent that saved the life of Nandi, Lord Shiva's bull. The region is also home to several ancient temples, including the famous Dilwara Jain Temples, known for their intricate marble carvings, which attract pilgrims and tourists alike. Wikipedia +2
